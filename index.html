Projects

Analyzing Bitcoin Price Fluctuations Through Tweets
Data Analysis Project
Project Report

In this project, I examined the powerful connection between Elon Musk's tweets and Bitcoin price volatility. By analyzing tweets from 2022 and applying sentiment analysis with TextBlob, I quantified the market's response within 24 hours of each tweet. My findings revealed that Musk's positive tweets typically led to Bitcoin price spikes, while negative ones triggered declines, confirming the hypothesis of significant social media influence on financial markets. This study highlights the outsized impact a single influential figure can have on investor sentiment and cryptocurrency trends.

Predicting NY Property Prices Through Income and Features
Project Report

In this project, I explored how average income and property features influence real estate prices across New York counties. By merging datasets from the U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis, World Population Review, and Kaggle, I built a comprehensive view of housing markets. I applied multiple linear regression models, starting with income and county data, then progressively enhancing the model with features like square footage and log transformations. My final model, incorporating income, property size, and county data, achieved an R-squared of 0.596, highlighting the significant role of property characteristics in price prediction.

Deplatforming and the Decline of Misinformation: A Twitter Case Study
Project Report

In this project, I explored whether deplatforming after January 6th effectively reduced misinformation on Twitter. Using a Difference-in-Differences (DiD) approach, I analyzed a comprehensive dataset tracking fake and non-fake posts over time, focusing on user groups like "QAnon" and suspended accounts. The results revealed a significant decline in misinformation sharing post-deplatforming, especially among the targeted user groups, with a marked drop in retweets and post initiations. The study underscored the potential of deplatforming as a tool to curb misinformation while raising important questions about content moderation.

This project demonstrates my ability to apply machine learning and NLP methods for in-depth text analysis.

Wine Clusters Uncorked: Dimensionality Reduction Meets K-Means
Project Report

This project explored unsupervised learning on the Wine dataset, focusing on clustering and dimensionality reduction. I standardized the data and applied K-means clustering to group wines based on their chemical properties. Then, I used Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to reduce the dataset's dimensionality while preserving variance. I visualized the clusters before and after PCA and compared clustering performance using silhouette scores to understand how dimensionality reduction impacts clustering quality.
